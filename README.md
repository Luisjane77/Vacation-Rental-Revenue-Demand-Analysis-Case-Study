This project presents an integrated case study on vacation rental performance, demand behavior, customer segmentation, and predictive analysis. It brings together exploratory analysis, dashboard-based insights, and machine learning workflows to understand how customer behavior and rental patterns can support stronger commercial and revenue-related decisions.

Rather than presenting isolated analyses, this repository consolidates multiple analytical components into a single business case focused on the vacation rental sector. The goal is to show how descriptive analysis, segmentation, predictive modeling, and visual reporting can be connected to generate practical insights for pricing, demand planning, and strategic decision-making.

---

## Business Context

Vacation rental businesses operate in highly dynamic environments shaped by seasonality, customer behavior, booking patterns, property characteristics, and market demand. In this context, understanding performance is not only about knowing how many bookings were generated, but also about identifying what type of customers book, how they behave, what drives pricing and performance, and how these patterns can support better commercial decisions.

This case study focuses on vacation rental data linked to the short-term rental market, combining business-oriented analysis with technical modeling. It is designed to reflect the kind of analytical thinking that can support operators, revenue teams, and commercial managers in understanding demand and improving performance.

---

## Project Objectives

The main objectives of this case study are:

- Analyze customer behavior in the vacation rental market
- Identify relevant booking and rental patterns
- Explore segmentation and behavioral clusters
- Understand the factors influencing rental pricing and performance
- Use predictive modeling techniques to extract actionable insights
- Support stronger commercial and revenue-related decision-making through data

## Data Scope

This case study combines analytical work developed around the vacation rental market, including:

- a business-focused customer behavior analysis centered on a vacation rental company in Benidorm during high season,
- predictive analysis based on short-term rental data from Barcelona,
- dashboard-based segmentation outputs designed to identify behavioral patterns and rental profiles.

Because some analytical blocks come from different but related rental datasets, this repository should be understood as an integrated case study in the broader vacation rental domain rather than as a single-source production dataset. The objective is to demonstrate business reasoning, technical analysis, and strategic interpretation within the same portfolio project.

## Exploratory Analysis
Main file:
- [Exploratory Analysis Notebook](02_exploratory_analysis/Untitled2.ipynb)
The exploratory phase focuses on understanding the vacation rental business from a descriptive and behavioral perspective.

Key themes explored include:

- customer behavior in high season,
- booking and rental patterns,
- revenue concentration,
- demand-related indicators,
- customer characteristics relevant to business performance.

This part of the project aims to answer practical business questions such as:

- Who are the most relevant customers?
- Which patterns appear in the strongest periods of activity?
- What type of rental behavior appears most valuable?
- What descriptive insights can support pricing or commercial decisions?

The exploratory layer provides the business foundation for the more advanced segmentation and predictive analysis developed in later sections.

## Segmentation Analysis

The segmentation component focuses on identifying groups, profiles, or behavioral clusters that help explain variation in rental behavior.

This part is especially useful for:

- understanding differences across customer types,
- identifying patterns that may not be obvious in aggregate metrics,
- supporting more tailored commercial or pricing decisions,
- translating data into customer-oriented insights.

The dashboard-based segmentation work helps turn technical outputs into a format that is easier to interpret from a business perspective.
