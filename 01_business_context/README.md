# Business Context

This section defines the business context behind the case study.

The project is positioned within the vacation rental sector, where performance depends on a combination of demand behavior, customer characteristics, rental patterns, seasonality, and property-level differences. In this environment, analytical work is valuable not only for describing what happened, but also for supporting stronger commercial understanding.

This case study was designed to connect technical analysis with business relevance. Rather than treating the project as a purely academic exercise, the intention is to frame it as a practical analytical case that can support thinking around demand, customer behavior, pricing-related interpretation, and overall rental performance.

The project also reflects a portfolio objective: showing how multiple analytical components developed across different repositories can be reorganized into one coherent vacation rental business case.

## Main Focus Areas

- Customer behavior in the vacation rental market
- Rental demand and performance patterns
- Segmentation and behavioral differences
- Predictive analysis of rental-related variables
- Revenue-related and commercial implications
